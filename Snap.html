Term
Definition
Management

RTOG1

Erythema (redness) and Mild pain
  • Use a salt rinse mouthwash
  • Gentle toothbrushing
  • Managing with low level pain relief e.g. paracetamol
RTOG2
Patchy Mucositis (inflammation of the gums, white patches) and Moderate pain.
  • Good hygeine routine
  • Soluable aspirin (analgesia/painkiller) gargle
  • Softer diet

RTOG3

Confluent Mucositis (joined large patches) Mucositis (inflammaiton of the gums, white patches) and severe pain.
  • Opiod pain relief (liquid morphine)
  • Pain relief Patches
  • Liquid diet or use a feeding tube

Lymphoedema

Swelling (tissue fluid built up in limbs)
  • Exercise to encourage flow of tissue fluid
  • Compression Bandages

Odynophagia

Pain on swallowing
  • Swallow Lignocaine Hydrocortisone ( a topical analgesic)

Moist Desquamation

Weeping Skin
  • Cover with a non-sticky dressing to promote granulation of tissue.
  • Stop using a moisturiser in that area

Xerostomia

Dry Mouth (due to lack of saliva production)
  • Use a 'dry mouth' mouthwash
  • Provision of Saliva substitutes

Dysphagia

Difficulty Swallowing
  • Softer diet (liquid/nil by mouth in extreme cases)
